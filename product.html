Hand-Transmitted vibration Analyser


Hand-Transmitted vibration (HTV) is a vibration transmitted to the hand and arm when handheld power tools and equipment are used. The regular use of vibrating tools can expose workers to mechanical oscillations, leading to potential health risks. HAVS, or hand-Transmitted vibration syndrome, is a condition associated with prolonged HTV exposure, characterized by symptoms such as numbness, tingling, and reduced dexterity in the hands and arms. The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) has established a comprehensive framework to address HAV exposure, with ISO 5349 being a pivotal standard in this regard. ISO standards provide vital parameters for measuring and managing HAV exposure, including frequency- weighted acceleration (r.m.s.), which accounts for variations in the sensitivity of the human hand-arm system to different vibration frequencies and calculation of the daily Exposure A(8), which signifies the daily vibration dose over an eight-hour workday and plays a critical role in establishing exposure limits. Compliance with these ISO standards ensures that HAV exposure is accurately measured, health risks are effectively managed, and control measures are implemented to protect the well-being of workers who routinely use handheld power tools and equipment in various occupational contexts.





Whole body vibration Analyzer

Whole-body vibration (WBV) is a type of mechanical oscillation that affects the entire human body, typically transmitted through the feet or the base of the spine. Prolonged exposure to WBV can result in health issues, including musculoskeletal problems, circulatory disturbances, and discomfort. To address this issue, the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) has established a comprehensive set of parameters and guidelines under ISO 2631. These essential metrics, including frequency-weighted acceleration and vibration dose value (VDV) serve as the cornerstone for evaluating and managing the effects of WBV on the human body. By adhering to these ISO standards, industries can ensure accuratemeasurement of WBV exposure, effectively mitigate health risks, and implement control measures to protect the safety and health of workers and individuals exposed to WBV in various occupational and environmental contexts.
